Summary

In this exploratory data analysis, we investigated the implications of property flipping and corporate ownership on Boston's residential housing market from 2000 to 2023. Key findings include:

  1. Flipping Properties: Investors generally hold properties for 1-2 years before flipping, with some transactions resulting in financial losses. Flipped properties are typically more expensive, suggesting they are often luxury properties.
  2. Corporate Ownership: An increasing trend in corporate ownership correlates inversely with the rate of owner-occupied residences. This shift is particularly noticeable in neighborhoods closer to the city center, where corporate ownership rates are higher and owner-occupancy rates are lower.
  3. Demographic Impact: Areas with higher corporate ownership tend to have older and predominantly white populations. However, due to the lack of updated racial data over time, it was challenging to establish if the demographic shifts are directly caused by corporate ownership or if these areas were already appealing to corporations for their existing demographic profiles.
